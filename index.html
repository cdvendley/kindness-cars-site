Kindness Cars Project

How it started

Fisher visits Seattle Children’s Hospital more than most kids. Since his very first appointment, he has always brought along a collection of toy cars to play with. Over time, he started sharing them with other kids he met and even giving away a few from his own collection.

Eventually, Fisher came up with an idea: instead of giving away his personal cars, why not bring brand-new ones to gift to other kids in the waiting room? What started as a simple act of kindness has become one of the highlights of his doctor visits.

Today, the Kindness Cars Project is how Fisher hopes to keep spreading joy to kids in unfamiliar and often scary clinical settings, one small car at a time.

What we do

We are just getting started. So far, friends and family have been donating new toy cars, and Fisher has been handing them out during his trips to the hospital. On his last visit alone, he gave away more than 20 cars.

Our hope is that Fisher’s small gesture keeps spreading. We want to encourage other families to start a similar sharing and gifting practice in the places where kids may need it most. We have seen firsthand that the children giving the cars often get just as much, or even more, joy out of the experience as the children receiving them.

A simple spontaneous gift can bring smiles, joy, and connection in a clinical setting. Fisher is the visionary behind this idea, and his family is here to support his vision as we work to keep it going and grow it beyond our small start.

Start your own gifting practice

We encourage others to start their own gifting tradition when they go to the hospital. Bring a few new toy cars, share them with children in waiting areas (when permitted), and invite your community to do the same.
